    Overview

    The Nuclear Test-Ban treaty is known for banning all nuclear tests that where underwater, in the atmosphere, or in space. It was signed by the United States, Great Britian, and the Soviet Union (Now known as Russia).

    It began when the U.S dropped the two atomic bombs in efforts to make Imperial Japan surrender during World War II. It successfully drew World War II to an end. 10 years, after World War II a huge public concern started to form, people were worrying about the consequences of nuclear fallout that were caused by the testing of nuclear weapons on the surface of the Earth. So, negotiations were discussed between the U.S., The Soviet Union, United Kingdom, Canada, and France. The two countries temporarily halted testing until they started testing due to the Cold War tensions between U.S. and Soviet Union two years later. However, an agreement between the two countries would speed up as a result of the Cuban missile crisis. The crisis showed the dangers of nuclear confrontation.

    The two countries had finally drafted a treaty. And after 10 days of discussion in Moscow, three representatives of the three nuclear powers, United States; Dean Rusk, United Kingdom; Sir Douglas Home, Union of Soviet Socialist Republics; A. Gromyko.

    Articles of the Treaty

Article number Summary of Article
Article I States that the Parties to this treatment should prohibit, prevent, and to not carry out any nuclear test explosion and/or any nuclear explosion under its jurisdiction that includes: outer space, high seas, territorial waters, underwater, and the atmosphere; beyond its limits. It also states that the Parties to this Treaty to refrain from causing, encouraging, or in any way participating in, the carrying out of any nuclear weapon test explosion, or any other nuclear explosion, anywhere which will take place in environments described above.
Article II A Party may propose amendments to this treaty, in where the amendment will be submitted to the Depositary Governments which will share the amendment to all Parties. The Depositary Governments will then assemble a conference if requested to do so by one-third or more of the Parties. All Parties will attend this conference. Any amendment to this Treaty must be approved by a majority of the vote of all the Parties to this Treaty, this includes the Original Parties. The amendment, if approved, will enter into force for all Parties upon the deposit of instrument of ratification by a Majority, this includes the all of the Original Parties.
Article III The Treaty is open to all States for signature. Any state that doesn't sign the Treaty before its entry into force to accord with entering into force after its ratification by all the Original Parties and the deposit of their instruments of ratifications. This Treaty will be subject to ratification by signatory States. Instruments of ratification and accession will be deposited with the Governments of the Original Parties; The United States of America, The United Kingdom, and the USSR—which are the Depositary Governments. States that deposited subsequent instruments or ratification or accession into force for this treaty shall enter force on the deposit of the instruments. The Depositary Governments will promptly inform all signatory and acceding States of the date of each signature and date of deposit of each instrument, it will be used if requested in a conference or other notices. The Treaty will be registered pursuant to Article 102 of the Chater of the United Nations.
Article IV This Treaty has an unlimited duration and cannot ‘expire’. Thus, each Party has a right in withdrawing from the Treaty willing fully if it has caused an extraordinary event related to the subject in this treaty and if the Treaty has ruined the high interests of its country by a three-month notice.
Article V The texts of the U.S., UK, and USSR will be deposited in the archives of Depositary Governments. Copies of this text will then be transferred to signatory and acceding States.
